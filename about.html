About

A quiet room for old paintings

I started Studio Fujisaki because my own screens felt loud. I wanted wallpapers that receded — that let a desktop breathe instead of shouting at me. The Japandi look, where Japanese restraint meets Scandinavian calm, was exactly that feeling, so I went looking for it in the paintings I already loved.

I'm not making these for some imagined audience out there. I'm one of them, so I make the wallpapers I'd want on my own screens — the phone I check first thing in the morning, the laptop I work on, the wide monitor I unwind in front of. If a piece doesn't earn a place on my own devices, it doesn't go up here.

Everything here is drawn from public-domain Japanese art: Edo-period woodblock prints, ink landscapes, folding-screen gardens. These works have outlived their painters by centuries and now belong to everyone. My job is small but deliberate — to choose the pieces, crop them kindly, and group them by mood rather than by museum.

How I curate

I sort by colour and feeling into three collections — Ink & Mist, Sage & Stone, and Warm Sand — so you can pick by the atmosphere you want rather than by knowing the art history. Each wallpaper keeps its proper credit: the artist, the era, and the museum that holds the original. I never burn text onto the image itself; the credit lives on the page, where it belongs.

Free, and free to stay quiet

The wallpapers are free to download for personal use. If a set makes your day a little calmer and you'd like to say thanks, there's a Ko-fi link in the footer — entirely optional, always appreciated.

— The person behind Studio Fujisaki
