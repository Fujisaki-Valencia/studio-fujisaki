About

A quiet room for quiet wallpapers

I started Studio Fujisaki because my own screens felt loud. I wanted wallpapers that receded — that let a desktop breathe instead of shouting at me. The Japandi look, where restraint meets a warm, Scandinavian calm, was exactly that feeling, so I started gathering the images that carried it.

I'm not making these for some imagined audience out there. I'm one of them, so I make the wallpapers I'd want on my own screens — the phone I check first thing in the morning, the laptop I work on, the wide monitor I unwind in front of. If a piece doesn't earn a place on my own devices, it doesn't go up here.

There's no single genre or source behind these. What ties them together is a feeling, not a category — muted colour, soft light, room to breathe. If an image brings that calm to a screen, it belongs here; if it doesn't, it stays out.

How I curate

I choose by colour and mood rather than by label, so you can pick the atmosphere you want without needing to know where a piece came from. Where a wallpaper has a known maker or source, I credit it on its page — and I never burn text onto the image itself. The credit lives on the page, where it belongs.

Free, and free to stay quiet

The wallpapers are free to download for personal use. If a set makes your day a little calmer and you'd like to say thanks, there's a Ko-fi link in the footer — entirely optional, always appreciated.

— The person behind Studio Fujisaki
